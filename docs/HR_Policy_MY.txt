HR POLICY — MALAYSIA RETAIL CHAIN
Document Title: Human Resources Policy & Employee Handbook (Summary)
Document ID: HR-POL-MY-001
Version: 2.0
Effective Date: 2024-01-01
Owner: Human Resources Department (HQ)
Applies To: All employees (Permanent, Contract, Part-Time) across Malaysia
Confidentiality: Internal Use Only

1) PURPOSE
This policy defines minimum HR standards for employment terms, leave, attendance, overtime, claims, conduct, performance, and disciplinary actions for a Malaysia-based retail chain. It supports fair treatment, legal compliance, operational consistency, and workforce wellbeing.

2) DEFINITIONS
- Permanent Employee: Full-time employee with confirmed employment after probation.
- Contract Employee: Fixed-term employee (e.g., 6–24 months) with contract end date.
- Part-Time Employee: Employee with reduced weekly hours as stated in offer letter.
- Probation: Initial employment period to assess suitability for confirmation.
- Supervisor: Direct reporting manager / outlet manager / department head.
- Medical Certificate (MC): Certified documentation by registered medical practitioner.

3) EMPLOYMENT CATEGORIES & PROBATION
3.1 Employment Types
a) Permanent (Full-Time)
b) Contract (Full-Time)
c) Part-Time / Casual (as per approved headcount plan)

3.2 Probation
- Permanent employees: 3 months probation (extendable up to 6 months based on performance).
- Contract employees: as stated in contract (typical 1–3 months).
- Confirmation requires satisfactory attendance, conduct, and performance.

4) WORKING HOURS & SHIFT MANAGEMENT
4.1 Standard Working Hours (Retail Operations)
- Retail outlets operate on shift basis, including weekends and public holidays.
- Typical shift patterns: 8 hours work + 1 hour meal break (subject to roster).
- Employees must follow roster schedules communicated by supervisors.

4.2 Rostering and Changes
- Rosters are published at least 7 days in advance when possible.
- Shift swaps require supervisor approval prior to change.
- Repeated last-minute changes without approval may be treated as attendance misconduct.

5) ATTENDANCE, PUNCTUALITY & ABSENCE
5.1 Attendance Expectations
- Employees must report on time for each shift.
- Timekeeping is recorded via approved system (e.g., biometric/clock-in/app).

5.2 Late Arrival
- Late arrival >10 minutes must be reported immediately to supervisor.
- Habitual lateness may trigger counselling and progressive discipline.

5.3 Unplanned Absence (No Show)
- If unable to attend, employee must notify supervisor before shift start (minimum 2 hours where possible).
- Failure to notify (“No Call No Show”) may result in disciplinary action.

6) LEAVE POLICY
6.1 Annual Leave (AL)
- Permanent Employees: 14 days per calendar year (prorated based on join date).
- Contract Employees: prorated annual leave per contract terms (minimum as per company offer).
- Part-Time: not eligible unless stated in employment terms.
- Leave approval is subject to business needs and manpower coverage.

6.2 Sick Leave (SL)
- Requires medical certificate (MC) for paid sick leave.
- MC must be submitted within 48 hours (or next working day for outlet submission).
- Abuse of MC may result in investigation.

6.3 Emergency Leave
- Granted at supervisor discretion for genuine emergencies.
- Employee may be required to submit supporting documents.

6.4 Unpaid Leave (UPL)
- May be approved for exceptional reasons when paid leave is exhausted.
- Subject to business needs and HR approval.

6.5 Maternity / Paternity / Compassionate Leave
- Provided according to Malaysian legal requirements and company guidelines.
- Employees should consult HR for eligibility and documentation.

7) OVERTIME (OT) POLICY
7.1 Eligibility
- Generally applicable to non-executive operational roles.
- Executive/managerial roles are normally not eligible unless explicitly approved.

7.2 OT Approval
- OT must be pre-approved by supervisor (written approval or roster instruction).
- OT without approval may not be payable.

7.3 OT Payment / Allowance
- OT is compensated according to company guidelines and applicable legal minimum requirements.
- HR/Payroll will calculate OT based on verified attendance records.

8) MEDICAL BENEFITS & CLAIMS
8.1 Medical Claim Entitlement (Outpatient)
- Permanent Employees: outpatient + specialist claims up to RM 300/month.
- Contract Employees: outpatient claims up to RM 150/month.
- Part-Time: claim eligibility subject to contract terms (default: not eligible unless specified).

8.2 Claim Submission Rules
- Claims must be submitted within 14 calendar days from treatment date.
- Required documents:
  a) Original receipt
  b) Clinic/hospital name and registration number
  c) Employee name
  d) Date of treatment
- Non-compliant submissions may be rejected.

8.3 Non-Claimable Items (Examples)
- Cosmetic / aesthetic treatments
- Non-medically necessary supplements
- Claims without valid receipts/MC documentation

9) CODE OF CONDUCT & ETHICS
9.1 Professional Behaviour
Employees must maintain respectful communication and professional conduct with customers, colleagues, and vendors.

9.2 Integrity & Fraud
- Dishonesty, theft, falsification of documents, or fraud is a serious offence.
- Examples include: fake claims, manipulating attendance, unauthorized discounts, cash mishandling.

9.3 Conflict of Interest
Employees must declare conflicts of interest (e.g., supplier relationships) to HR/Management.

9.4 Confidentiality
Company information, customer data, and internal documents must not be shared externally without approval.

10) PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
10.1 Performance Review
- Supervisors conduct performance check-ins at least twice a year.
- Performance criteria may include: sales discipline, customer service, attendance, teamwork, compliance, and role-specific KPIs.

10.2 Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Issued when performance is below expectations.
- PIP includes clear goals, timeline (typically 30–90 days), and review checkpoints.
- Failure to improve may lead to further action including termination (subject to due process).

11) DISCIPLINARY PROCESS (PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE)
11.1 Principles
The company applies fair investigation, documentation, and the right to be heard.

11.2 Typical Steps (depending on severity)
a) Verbal Counselling (documented)
b) Written Warning (First)
c) Final Written Warning
d) Termination (for repeated misconduct or serious offences)

11.3 Serious Misconduct (may result in immediate action)
Examples include:
- Theft / fraud
- Physical violence or threats
- Harassment or discrimination
- Severe insubordination
- Major safety violations
- “No Call No Show” repeatedly

12) GRIEVANCE & WHISTLEBLOWING
- Employees may raise concerns through supervisor, HR, or official reporting channels.
- Reports are treated confidentially and investigated fairly.
- Retaliation against whistleblowers is prohibited.

13) DATA PRIVACY
Employee personal data is processed for employment administration. Access is restricted to authorized personnel. Data handling follows company procedures and applicable privacy requirements.

14) DOCUMENT CONTROL
- This policy may be updated by HR with management approval.
- The latest version supersedes previous versions.
- Employees are responsible for staying updated on current policies.
